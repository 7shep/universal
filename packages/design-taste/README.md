# Design taste policy

`@universal/design-taste` is a deterministic, local-only design-quality guardrail, not a
style template. It gives planning and review a shared vocabulary for specificity,
coherence, restraint, accessibility, and intentional rationale without prescribing a
dark palette, animation, unusual navigation, or any other aesthetic device.

Positive reference signals are abstracted into principles and notes; no layouts,
assets, copy, code, or brand identifiers are copied. Anti-patterns are review warnings,
not blanket bans, and may be reduced when a plan records a credible, matching reason.
Structural and accessibility failures remain failures regardless of taste score.

The policy operates from source, plan data, screenshot presence, and structured review
observations. Pixel-level visual critique is intentionally deferred until the
generated-project preview loop is complete.
